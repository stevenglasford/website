But Minneapolis was chosen due to its progressiveness revitalization from the death of George Floyd, its centralization to friends and family to Steven. Then Steven started to only travel via Amtrak. He made regular commutes from MSP to New York City, which taught him and allowed him to meet many different people. Steven would take different routes often times and on one east-bound route from Chicago to Washington DC he met several representatives from Green Peace. After a long discussion the representative thought the best thing would be to reduce the amount of oil being pumped out of the ground. So Steven began researching more about PLA plastics, which are typcially sourced from American grown corn. It takes about 2kg of raw corn to produce 1kg of PLA plastic, so it is extremly efficient. Many critics often argue about the environmental benefits of PLA plastic, such critism is as follows: 1. The creation of PLA plastic reduces the food supply. 2. Current generation techniques for generating PLA plastic use carbon (such as tractors). 3. PLA takes about 80 years to decompose naturally. We at glasford.io do not see any problems with the above for the following reasons: 1. Most corn produced in America goes towards animal feed and ethanol production, PLA plastic will never take as much resources as either of these industries. 2. America is already infested with cars so this is true with every single industry and person in America. 3. 80 years to decompose is significantly faster than the 1000+ years needed to break down most other plastics. The problems of PLA are often conflated and exagerated by the plastic industry in an effort to sell more polution causing plastic. That is the official stance and opinion of glasford.io. The conversation with Green Peace also resulted in many other standard practices at glasford.io. Many other people also provided more ideas, we at glasford.io thank all others that had converations with Steven over the time.
